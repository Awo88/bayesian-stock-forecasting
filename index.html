Bayesian Stock

Probabilistic equity price forecasting using Bayesian inference, posterior distributions, and real financial market data.

Overview

This project applies Bayesian statistical methods to stock price forecasting — moving beyond point estimates to model full probability distributions over future returns. By incorporating prior beliefs and updating them with observed market data, the model produces posterior distributions that quantify uncertainty in a way traditional forecasting cannot.

Built entirely in R, the analysis draws on real financial market data and outputs probabilistic forecasts with credible intervals rather than single-value predictions.

Methods
Bayesian Inference
Prior beliefs updated with observed data to produce posterior distributions over model parameters
Posterior Distributions
Full probability distributions over future price movements rather than single-point forecasts
Credible Intervals
Probabilistic uncertainty bands on forecasts, quantifying confidence at each time horizon
Market Data Analysis
Real financial price series used for model training, validation, and out-of-sample testing
